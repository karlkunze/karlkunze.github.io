\hypertarget{experience}{%
\section{Experience}\label{experience}}

\hypertarget{management-of-a-winter-malting-barley-breeding-program-for-new-york-state}{%
\subsection{Management of a winter malting barley breeding program for
New York
State}\label{management-of-a-winter-malting-barley-breeding-program-for-new-york-state}}

\begin{itemize}
\item
  Advanced a double haploid and recombinant inbred population starting
  with crosses in 2017. Currently testing 25 lines for a third year of
  yield trials and a preliminary yield trial consisting of 200 advanced
  double haploid lines and 200 recombinant inbred lines.
\item
  Micro malted 240 winter barley over two time points at the USDA Cereal
  crops and research unit in Madison, WI. Malting quality resulted were
  used to inform selection decisions
  \hfill\mbox{Dec 2021 and Jan-Feb 2022}
\item
  Used genomic prediction, linear mixed models, spatial correction and
  genome wide association studies to advance lines and inform selection
  decisions
\end{itemize}

\hypertarget{organic-multi-use-naked-barley-for-new-york-and-the-northern-united-states}{%
\subsection{Organic Multi-use naked barley for New York and the Northern
United
States}\label{organic-multi-use-naked-barley-for-new-york-and-the-northern-united-states}}

\begin{itemize}
\item
  Advanced a small population of covered by naked and naked by naked
  winter barley crosses to develop a naked barley variety adapted to New
  York conditions.
\item
  Performed genotype by environment analysis for eight naked barley
  trials across the northern United States
\item
  Performed a genome wide association analysis of 250 diverse lines
  across 13 environments for foliar disease resistance in natural field
  organic conditions
\item
  Used aerial imaging of a spring naked barley variety trial over two
  years to quantify barley growth over time as a precursor for
  competition with weeds.
\item
  Initialized a combined nested association population using three
  common parents across all collaborating institution and five parents
  specific to NY conditions.
\end{itemize}
